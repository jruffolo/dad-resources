Therapy Tools

  • Aphasia Therapy Online (website, free)
    Very simple interface. Select from Listening, Reading, Spelling, and Naming exercises. Once you begin an exercise, you can hit the Settings button to change the word difficulty, theme, region, and more. Click here for more information
  • Lingraphica TalkPath (website and app, free)
    Navigate to "All Exercises" to choose from a wide variety of exercises, divided into subcategories and difficulty levels. UI is a bit outdated. The mobile app is available to download here

Visual Dictionaries

  • Language Guide
    A simple website with a good representation of common words separated by category. Could be very useful to find the right word you're looking for, but also to practice with its speaking challenges. Navigate to a category and hover over / click on the picture to see the word and play its corresponding audio file.
  • ESOL Online Picture Dictionary
    An old website made for language learners. Has many different categories with common words, including an "actions" category which the other dictionaries lack. Unfortunately it constantly prompts you to download Adobe Flash player which you have to dismiss each time.
  • Ikonet Visual Dictionary
    A very detailed dictionary with many subcategories. Somewhat scientific and maybe too complex to use quickly, but it still could be useful. Old website, works better on desktop than mobile. There is a paid app version available to download here
